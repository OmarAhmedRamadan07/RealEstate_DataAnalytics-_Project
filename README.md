## 🎯 Executive Summary
Despite high demand in the New York City short-term rental and real estate market, many property owners (hosts) face inconsistent revenue and low occupancy rates. This project analyzes a dataset of over 45,000 listings to identify the root causes of underperformance and provides a data-driven, interactive tool to optimize pricing and booking strategies.

We transition from basic descriptive statistics to prescriptive analytics by introducing an **AI-driven Strategy Simulator** that instantly diagnoses property parameters against dynamic market benchmarks.

---

## 💡 Business Context (Problem & Solution)

* **What is the problem?** A significant gap in listing performance across different NYC boroughs. Properties remain vacant despite seemingly competitive pricing due to hidden barriers like strict minimum stay requirements or poor market positioning.
* **What does the data show?** Listings with `minimum_nights` > 3 suffer a severe drop in review density (engagement). Furthermore, properties priced >20% above their specific neighborhood average have drastically lower occupancy indicators.
* **Why is the problem happening?** Hosts lack context-aware benchmarks. They price based on global NYC averages rather than hyperlocal neighborhood data, leading to overpricing in competitive zones.
* **What should be done?** Implement a flexible booking policy (1-2 nights minimum) and adjust prices dynamically based on specific borough averages. We built a Streamlit Dashboard to automate this advisory process for hosts.

---

## 🚀 Key Features

1. **Context-Aware Rule-Based System:** A custom algorithm that assigns specific, actionable recommendations (e.g., "Lower price", "Reduce minimum nights") to every listing.
2. **Interactive Dashboard:** Built with Streamlit, providing real-time filtering by Borough, Room Type, and Price Range.
3. **AI Strategy Simulator:** A proactive tool for new hosts. Users can input hypothetical property details (Name, Borough, Target Price, Min Nights) and instantly receive a strategic diagnosis comparing their inputs to the actual market averages.
